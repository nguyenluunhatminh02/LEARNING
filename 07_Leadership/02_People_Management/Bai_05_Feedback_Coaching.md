# Bài 05: 1:1s, Feedback & Coaching

## 🎯 Mục tiêu
- Running effective 1:1 meetings
- Giving & receiving feedback (Radical Candor)
- Performance management & reviews
- Career development conversations

## 📖 Câu chuyện đời thường
> **1:1** giống buổi cà phê với bạn thân: không phải báo cáo mà là nói chuyện thật — "đang gặp khó khăn gì?", "cần giúp gì?". **Radical Candor** là cân bằng giữa quan tâm và thẳng thắn. Nói "bài thuyết trình chưa tốt vì lý do X, thử cách Y" tốt hơn "bài tốt lắm" (giả tạo) hoặc "bài dở quá" (tầm hười). Giống bạn thân nói thật: "chiếc váy này không hợp với bạn" — vì quan tâm nên mới nói thật. **Career conversation** là hỏi: "5 năm nữa bạn muốn làm gì?" rồi giúp họ đi đúng hướng.

## 1. Effective 1:1 Meetings

```
1:1 = THEIR meeting, not yours

Purpose:
  - Build trust & relationship
  - Uncover problems early
  - Coach & develop
  - Remove blockers
  - NOT status updates (use standups/async for that)

Structure (30 min weekly):
  5 min — Check-in: "How are you? Anything on your mind?"
  15 min — Their topics: "What would you like to discuss?"
  5 min — Your topics: Feedback, context, upcoming changes
  5 min — Action items: "What did we decide? Next steps?"

Good Questions:
  Engagement:
    "What's the most interesting thing you worked on this week?"
    "What's frustrating you right now?"
    "If you could change one thing about our team, what would it be?"
  
  Growth:
    "What skill would you like to develop this quarter?"
    "What type of work energizes you?"
    "Where do you see yourself in 2 years?"
  
  Management:
    "Is there anything I should be doing differently?"
    "Do you feel you have enough context on priorities?"
    "Is there anything blocking you that I can help with?"

Anti-patterns:
  ❌ Skipping 1:1s when busy → signals "you're not important"
  ❌ Pure status update → waste of time, use Slack
  ❌ All talking, no listening → ratio goal: 70% them, 30% you
  ❌ No notes → forget commitments, repeat conversations
```

## 2. Feedback Framework — Radical Candor

```
Radical Candor = Care Personally + Challenge Directly

          Care Personally ↑
                │
  Ruinous      │  Radical
  Empathy      │  Candor ✅
  (nice but    │  (honest AND
   not honest) │   kind)
 ─────────────┼─────────────→ Challenge Directly
  Manipulative │  Obnoxious
  Insincerity  │  Aggression
  (neither     │  (honest but
   nice nor    │   cruel)
   honest)     │

Giving Feedback (SBI Model):
  S — Situation: "In yesterday's design review..."
  B — Behavior: "...you interrupted the presenter three times."
  I — Impact: "...which made them lose their train of thought
               and the team missed important context."

  Then ask: "What's your perspective?"
  And propose: "Next time, could you note questions and ask after?"

Timing Rules:
  Praise: Public or private (most people prefer public recognition)
  Criticism: ALWAYS private, NEVER in front of others
  Both: Give within 48 hours (not weeks later)

Be specific:
  ❌ "Great job!" → meaningless
  ✅ "Your API design doc was excellent — the alternatives section 
      made the decision clear for everyone."
  
  ❌ "You need to improve." → vague, demotivating
  ✅ "Your PR descriptions are too brief. Adding context about WHY 
      would help reviewers and future debugging."
```

## 3. Performance Management

```
Performance Review (quarterly or semi-annually):

No surprises rule: Everything in the review should have been
discussed in 1:1s already. Review = formal documentation.

Rating Framework:
  Significantly exceeds: Impact beyond their level, ready for promotion
  Exceeds:              Strong performance, goes above expectations
  Meets:                Good, solid contributor at their level
  Below:                Not meeting expectations, needs improvement plan

Writing Performance Reviews:
  Structure per area:
  1. What they did well (specific examples)
  2. Areas for growth (specific, actionable)
  3. Impact on team/org

  ❌ "Team player, works hard, good coder"
  ✅ "Led the migration to K8s (3 months early), reducing deployment 
      time from 45min to 5min. The 20-page runbook they wrote enabled 
      3 other teams to self-serve. Growth area: presenting at all-hands 
      would increase visibility for well-deserved recognition."

Performance Improvement Plan (PIP):
  When: Consistent underperformance after coaching
  Structure:
    - Clear expectations (specific, measurable)
    - Timeline (30-60 days)
    - Support provided (mentoring, pair programming)
    - Check-in cadence (weekly)
    - Consequences (termination if not met)
  Goal: Genuinely help them improve OR make a fair exit
```

## 4. Career Development

```
Career Ladder Components:
  Level 1 (Junior):    Learns and delivers with support
  Level 2 (Mid):       Delivers independently
  Level 3 (Senior):    Leads features, mentors others
  Level 4 (Staff):     Drives technical direction, org-wide impact
  Level 5 (Principal): Shapes industry practice, company strategy

IC vs Manager Track:
  Both are valid, neither is "promotion" over the other.

  IC Track:    Senior → Staff → Principal → Distinguished
               Deep technical impact, architecture, mentoring
  
  Manager:     TL → EM → Director → VP → CTO
               People leadership, org design, strategy

Career Development 1:1 (quarterly):
  1. "Where do you want to be in 1-2 years?"
  2. "What skills gap exists between now and there?"
  3. "What projects/experiences would build those skills?"
  4. "How can I help? (sponsorship, stretch assignments, training)"

Development Actions:
  - Stretch assignments: Tasks slightly above current level
  - Exposure: Present at all-hands, lead cross-team project
  - Education: Conference, course, book club
  - Mentoring: Pair with someone who has the target skill
  - Writing: Blog posts, design docs, ADRs (builds communication)
```

## 5. Difficult Conversations

```
Framework for Hard Conversations:

Before:
  - State facts, not judgments
  - Prepare specific examples
  - Consider their perspective
  - Choose private, calm setting

During:
  1. State the issue clearly
     "I've noticed you've missed 3 sprint commitments in a row."
  2. Share impact
     "The team had to pick up the work, and we missed our deadline."
  3. Ask their perspective
     "Help me understand what's going on."
  4. Listen genuinely
     (Maybe: burnout, personal issues, unclear expectations)
  5. Agree on next steps
     "Let's try X for the next 2 weeks and check in."

After:
  - Follow up on commitments
  - Document the conversation
  - Acknowledge improvement when you see it

Key mindset:
  "I'm having this conversation BECAUSE I care about 
   your growth, not to punish you."
```
